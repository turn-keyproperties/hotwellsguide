Guide
Check-in & Check-out

Check-in is from 3:00pm. Check-out is by 10:00am. This is a self check-in — your key is in the lock box by the front door (code 6573). On the day you leave, please pop the key back in the lock box.

WiFi & Ethernet

The WiFi network name is VM7152957 and the password is mo9nhbLmcogm5utk. Every bedroom also has an ethernet port.

Keys

If you are locked out, there is a lock box with a spare key. The code is 6573.

Parking

If you have arrived by car and need a parking permit, please message the host via the booking platform with your car registration number.

Heating & Hot Water

The heating and hot water are set to come on twice a day (morning and evening), all controlled via the Hive setup. If you need more hot water, press the top right button on the thermostat.

Toilets (Top Two Floors)

The toilets on the top two floors are fitted with macerator systems, which help remove waste efficiently. To keep them working properly and avoid any issues during your stay, please only flush toilet paper and human waste. Anything else should be disposed of in the bins provided. Thank you for your understanding and help in looking after the property.

Washing Machine & Tumble Dryer

The washing machine and tumble dryer are in the ground-floor kitchen with the boiler. Feel free to use them as needed during your stay.

Waste & Recycling

Bin collection day is Thursday. If your stay includes a Wednesday night, we kindly ask that you place the bins out the evening before to help us keep everything running smoothly. If not, please simply leave any rubbish bagged in the kitchen bin and we'll take care of the rest. For recycling, please use the green box, black box and blue bag provided. Thank you for your help.

Water

If there is a water-related emergency, the stop-cock is in the plant room (with the washing machine) in the ground-floor kitchen. The points to turn the water on and off are marked with yellow labels in case of a leak.

Fire Alarm

If the alarm goes off, you can deal with it at the panel by the front door: press 1 (turns off the alarm), press 2 (turns off the buzzing), then press 3 (resets the system).

Smokeless Area

Hotwells is located in an area where restrictions apply to reduce smoke emissions, so we kindly ask that guests do not have any bonfires or similar open fires outside. Barbecues should be fine to use.

House Rules

To keep the house lovely for everyone, please: no smoking anywhere inside; no parties or events; observe quiet hours 10pm–8am out of respect for the neighbours; only booked guests may stay overnight; and no pets, please.

Before You Leave

Before checking out at 10:00am, we'd be grateful if you could place any used towels in the bathroom, bag any rubbish and leave it in the kitchen, switch off the lights, close any open windows, and return the key to the lockbox. Thank you for taking care of the house during your stay. We hope you've had a wonderful visit and wish you a safe journey home.

Getting Around

Hotwell Road is a busy bus corridor, so getting into town is easy. The simplest hop is a First Bus into The Centre, where you can pick up onward connections.

City Centre & Bus Station

Frequent First Bus services run along Hotwell Road into town — service 8 (Hotwells ↔ The Centre ↔ Temple Meads ↔ Clifton) is the handiest, roughly every 10 minutes and only a few minutes to The Centre. The Bristol Bus & Coach Station on Marlborough Street (for National Express coaches) is a short ride or a ~15-minute walk on from The Centre.

Bus 8 · ~5 min to The Centre

Bristol Temple Meads

Bristol's main intercity railway station. Take service 8 or the A1 Airport Flyer from Hotwell Road, or change in The Centre. A taxi takes around 10 minutes.

Bus 8 / A1 · ~15–20 min

Clifton Down Station

Your nearest local station, up the hill in Clifton on the Severn Beach line. Service 8 runs up to Clifton Village nearby, or it's a 15–20 minute uphill walk. Trains reach Temple Meads in about 15 minutes.

Bus 8 / on foot · ~15 min

Bristol Parkway

North of the city, best reached by rail. Get to Temple Meads first (service 8 or the A1), then a direct GWR train out to Parkway takes roughly 10–13 minutes.

Via Temple Meads · ~40 min total

Bristol Airport

The A1 Bristol Airport Flyer is the easy option — it runs 24 hours a day, up to every 8 minutes, from The Centre, the Bus Station and Temple Meads. Hop a bus into The Centre, then board the A1; the run out to the airport takes around 30 minutes.

A1 Flyer · ~30 min from The Centre

Tip: a contactless tap or the First Bus app covers the city buses; the Airport Flyer is a separate ticket. Times are guides — check live departures on the First Bus or Google Maps app before you set off.
